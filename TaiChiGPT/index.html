Abstract


Large language models (LLMs) have shown remarkable capabilities in text generation and understanding. In this research, we propose TaiChiGPT, a novel framework for complex sports action generation based on LLMs. Our approach leverages the knowledge and reasoning capabilities of large language models to generate realistic and diverse Tai Chi action sequences. The system incorporates domain-specific knowledge and motion constraints to ensure biomechanical plausibility and temporal consistency. Experimental results demonstrate that TaiChiGPT can generate high-quality action sequences that are indistinguishable from real Tai Chi performances.

Methodology




TaiChiGPT consists of three main components: (1) a prompt engineering module that translates natural language descriptions into structured action representations, (2) a motion generation module that ensures biomechanical constraints, and (3) a refinement module that enhances temporal consistency and visual quality. The framework integrates LLMs with traditional motion generation techniques to produce realistic sports actions.





Results





TaiChiGPT achieves state-of-the-art performance in action generation quality, outperforming existing methods in both objective metrics and human evaluation. The generated actions demonstrate smooth transitions, realistic motion patterns, and adherence to Tai Chi principles.





Applications




TaiChiGPT has wide applications in sports training, virtual coaching, and action composition. It can be used to generate reference demonstrations, create training materials, and assist in the development of AI-powered sports education platforms.
